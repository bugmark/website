Bugmark for Product Managers and Investors

Bugmark.net

The product manager in this system, in the hands-on manager of the software product - they configure or otherwise control the issue tracker, and other software management systems. The investor is someone who watches market trends and makes investments on issues or buys contracts.

Why should you invest in Bugmark?

Unlike Bug bounty systems : tradable contracts at market prices.

Unlike kicktstarter campaigns: finegrained contracts attached to Github issues.

Unlike social signals : a positive outlet for priorities and developer support.

How does it work?



Sign up on Bugmark.net with your Github account.

If you want to report issues, click on issues in the menu and report your issue with required details. Propose a smart cotract: "I will invest $18, if I can collect $20 on July 1 if bug 31337 is unfixed."

Vinod, a developer sees the smart contract proposal. He counter-offers: "I will invest $2 and collect $20 on July 1 if bug 31337 is fixed."

The smart contract is formed automatically. You invest $18.25 and Vinod invests $2.25.

Vinod fixes the bug and the project maintainer closes it. He will receive $20. The oracle that closed the smart contract receives $0.50. You lose the investment, but gain a feature, which has an incredibly higher value.

Liquidity Trades

If you own the fixed side of a contract(paying for a fix), and need to get your money out, you can sell your side of the contract partway through the fix (before the bug is entirely fixed, checked in, reviewed, etc.) - you will probably have to sell at a discount, effectively sponsoring the fix, during the time you held your side of the contract.

If the underlying task of a contract is verified completely, but contract maturation has not been reached, then the person who completed the task can check in, and offer to sell her side of the conntract - this is a no brainer for an investor to purchase, as the task has been verified - the fixer just wants the liquidity right away instead of later - should be easy to arrange and depending on the price this can be fair and amneable to all.

Tradable contracts are important for incentivising partial work, a key feature of Bugmark that futures markets can achieve but pure open source bounty systems have trouble with.

Bugmark vs Other Markets

Descentralized - implemented as an Ethereum Dapp.

Extensible by Researches - incentives can be modified and experimented with - easily!

Straightforward for Developers - to the extent possible, we'll use garden variety tech and write full test suites.

Unobtrusive - no changes should be required to existing project management systems (issue trackers, etc.), owners/managers of projects may participate as much or as little as desired.

Pseudononymous - we are after intelligence, and obtaining that info often requires a degree of respect for privacy.

Why Bugmark?
Testimonials Collaborations

  • Bugmark reduces feature costs

    Reduces costs related to adding a new feature, like time and monetary costs.

    • Price Signals

      Decision making is easier based on price signals, and the developer can easily decide which features should be given preference to.

    • Market influence

      Priorities easily visible and able to be influenced by funders with small time investment through trading.

    • Market Perspective

      Work on features, which are also directed by market / users, rather than the managers who are mostly not the target audience for the product.

  • Bugmark reduces recruitment cost

    In a way it is crowdsourced online, so it saves the time and effort invested in finding a new dev.

    • Market competition

      Since it's a market there will be competition - project manager can get better costs with us than by approaching a dev individually where the developer may have an upper hand in deciding the costs/compensation.

  • Bugmark reduces time to fix issues

    Since each offer has an expiry date, the developer accepts issues based on fcfs basis, further the contracts involve a maturity period, and the developer would only accept new positions/partial work which she/he can aim to get resolved in the given time period.

    • Task management

      Big projects involve a lot of tasks, investment prevents a developer from taking more issues than she/he can be accountable for. This ultimately leads to more issues getting resolved instead of partial unfixed work.

    • Partial Work

      Collaborative work is incentivised, this supports an efficient open source culture.

    • Information sharing

      Bugmark supports an open culture of sharing of information datasets for academicians, and open source collaboration.
